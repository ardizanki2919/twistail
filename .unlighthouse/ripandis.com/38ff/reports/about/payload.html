Hi! My name is Aris Ripandi

I live in Sukabumi, where I work remotely.

As an enthusiastic and self-driven professional, I possess an insatiable curiosity for learning and embracing new challenges. My deep fascination with cloud computing and modern web development drives my work, while I actively dedicate time to contributing to the Open Source community — a commitment that reflects my belief in collaborative innovation and knowledge sharing.

Interest

I have big interests in modern web technology, full-stack development, and cloud computing. So, expect to see more of this stuff in the future! Besides working at Zero One Group , I'm currently crafting web apps and diving into cloud computing technology. These days, my favorite stacks are React, Next.js, Remix, Prisma ORM, Drizzle ORM, Fastify, Laravel, and PostgreSQL.

Technical Skills

Proficient in PHP, Typescript, and a little bit of Java, but now I'm learning Rust. Developing modern web applications with a clean and maintainable codebase is one of my biggest passions.

Open Source enthusiast

In my spare time sometimes I create developer tools and libraries, some of them has been published as NPM, Composer and Crates packages. You can explore my GitHub repositories to see what I've been working on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Curious minds want to know: addressing the questions people frequently
ask about my work and expertise.

What kind of projects do you typically work on?

As a polyglot developer and web artisan, I work on a variety of projects including web applications, open source tools, and educational resources. I enjoy projects that challenge me to use different programming languages and technologies, allowing me to constantly learn and grow as a developer.

How do you balance being a lecturer and a developer?

It's all about passion and time management! Teaching lets me share knowledge while keeping me accountable to stay current with technology trends. My development work informs my teaching with real-world examples, and my students often inspire me with fresh perspectives. This symbiotic relationship between coding and teaching creates a fulfilling cycle of continuous learning.

What's your approach to open source contribution?

I believe in the collaborative spirit of open source. My approach is to contribute meaningfully to projects I regularly use, create tools that solve real problems I've encountered, and mentor newcomers to join the community. Open source is not just about code—it's about building connections, sharing knowledge, and making technology accessible to everyone.

How do you choose which technologies to learn or use in projects?

I follow a pragmatic approach when selecting technologies. First, I evaluate if it solves the problem effectively. Then I consider factors like community support, long-term viability, and how it fits into the existing ecosystem. Being a polyglot developer allows me to pick the right tool for each job rather than forcing one solution for everything. I also enjoy experimenting with emerging technologies to stay ahead of industry trends and bring innovative solutions to my projects and teaching.
